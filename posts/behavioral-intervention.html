Software as a Behavioural Intervention in Science

22 August 2025

Thesis: The most powerful thing software can do for science is not to save time, but to change behaviour—making rigour and reproducibility the default.

The problem

Reproducibility failures stem from weak hypotheses, misused statistics, and opaque protocols. Most ELNs record work; few improve it.

A design stance

  • Hypothesis discipline: pre-registration prompts; link experiments to hypotheses; track supported/refuted outcomes.
  • Statistical guardrails: suggest appropriate tests; detect pseudoreplication; power analysis before data collection.
  • Protocol standardisation: version control, attribution, modular templates—GitHub for wet lab.

Why now

LLMs make contextual nudges feasible; funders and journals demand transparency; cultural change can be shipped as UX.

Conclusion

Build tools that quietly enforce good science without stifling curiosity. That’s the opportunity.

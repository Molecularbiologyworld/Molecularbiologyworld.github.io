About Me

Welcome to my personal website! I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge. My current work focuses on understanding how defects in glycosphingolipid-processing enzymes change neuronal development and behaviour, and how these alterations link to human neurological disorders. My previous work explored the interface between protein misfolding in neurodegeneration, progeria syndromes, and nuclear architecture.

Beyond my primary research, I’m drawn to questions in artificial intelligence, philosophy, and the ethical challenges that arise in healthcare and biomedical research.

In my spare time I enjoy science fiction, music, Krav Maga, and HYROX training.

Research interests

  • Neuroscience: neurodegenerative diseases (especially Alzheimer’s and other protein-misfolding diseases), and glycosphingolipid-related disorders.
  • Ageing biology: progeria syndromes, epigenetics, cellular reprogramming, and rejuvenation.
  • AI: machine learning for structural biology and drug design.

Writing

Short essays on ethics in biological research, experimental design, and the culture of science.

Speaking & Teaching
